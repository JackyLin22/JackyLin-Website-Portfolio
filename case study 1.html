Creating a Secure Partner Portal for Primary Care Coalition

A healthcare UX capstone project focused on improving secure collaboration and document sharing between PCC staff and external partners.

View On Figma

Timeline

September 2025 – May 2026

Role

UX Researcher & Usability Testing Lead

Tools

Figma, SharePoint, Miro, Trello

Overview

Primary Care Coalition (PCC) is a nonprofit healthcare organization serving more than 30,000 uninsured and underserved individuals.

PCC needed a more secure and efficient way for staff and external healthcare partners to share documents and collaborate using SharePoint.

The Problem

Expired SharePoint Links

Staff repeatedly re-shared files because links expired unexpectedly.

Permission Complexity

Access management was confusing and often required IT support.

Security Concerns

Sensitive documents were shared through insecure email workflows.

Research

Our team conducted:

  • Background research
  • Competitive analysis
  • Stakeholder interviews
  • User journey mapping
  • Affinity diagramming
  • Usability testing

Research revealed the need for:

  • Centralized access to documents
  • Clearer permissions
  • Reduced administrative burden

Information Architecture

We organized the portal around four major program areas:

  • Administrative
  • Population Health
  • Healthcare Access
  • About PCC

The system used a two-tier model:

  • Public documents accessible without authentication
  • Private documents protected through authenticated access

Usability Testing

I planned and conducted usability sessions with PCC staff members across multiple departments.

Folder-Based Access Improved Usability

Participants strongly preferred persistent folder links over individual file links.

Information Architecture Increased Confidence

Users navigated more confidently when content reflected their mental models and workflows.

Governance Was Critical

Participants consistently raised concerns about permissions and access control.

Outcome

The project delivered:

  • Research-backed portal architecture
  • Validated mid and high-fidelity prototypes
  • Governance recommendations
  • SharePoint implementation guidance

What I Learned

This capstone project taught me how to balance user needs, security requirements, organizational goals, and technical constraints within a real healthcare environment.

I also learned that usability testing is about observing behavior rather than collecting opinions, and that information architecture directly shapes user confidence and workflow efficiency.
